About

I want to use knowledge to change the world for the better — and I believe that means following curiosity wherever it leads, across disciplines, communities, and languages.

I'm a linguistics student, singer, performer, choir director, researcher, and student council president based in Seoul, South Korea. My work lives at the intersection of language, identity, and community, from writing an Igbo language textbook to support children reconnecting with their roots to conducting research spanning Korean historical linguistics to translation cognition.

Whether I'm on stage, in the field, or deep in a corpus, I am driven by wonder for the unknown and a deep love for the world.
