Igbo Language Textbook

A textbook written from scratch to support heritage Igbo classes in Seoul — for children born in Korea who are reconnecting with the language and culture of their families.

[ Add photos of your drafts, the community, or textbook pages here ]

There is a community in Seoul whose children speak Korean and English fluently — but not Igbo, the language their families carry from Nigeria. Classes exist to teach them, but accessible, learner-appropriate materials do not. I'm writing the textbook those classes need.

The project requires working across three linguistic contexts at once: Igbo as the target language, Korean and English as the children's native languages, and the cultural knowledge embedded in the language that pure translation cannot capture. It is the most complex and meaningful thing I have ever made.

The textbook is structured to meet learners where they are — beginning with phonology and everyday vocabulary and building toward grammar, narrative, and cultural context. It is written to be used, not archived.

Community · Music Ongoing

Moonlight Children's Choir

A choir for refugee and migrant children in Seoul, which I founded and direct — built on the belief that music is one of the most honest languages we share.

[ Add photos of rehearsals, performances, or the children here ]

I founded Moonlight Children's Choir because I believe that children who have moved across borders — who carry more than one language, more than one home — deserve a space that honors that complexity. Music is a place where belonging doesn't require fluency.

The choir brings together children from refugee and migrant communities across Seoul. We rehearse regularly, perform publicly, and create something together that none of us could make alone. I direct, arrange, and sometimes write for the ensemble.

The name Moonlight comes from the idea that the moon is the same moon everywhere — a small thing, but it matters when you're far from home.

Student Lounge

A proposal to transform an unused school space into the first student-only lounge — a dedicated hub for rest, collaboration, and community.

[ Add proposal documents, mockups, or photos here as the project develops ]

Students at my school have limited options for informal gathering — the library is perpetually overcrowded and must function as both study and social space, and there is no dedicated place to simply rest or connect between classes. I proposed creating one.

The Student Lounge would transform an unused location — such as the upper conference hall or an unused classroom — into a comfortable, inclusive environment where students can relax, collaborate, and recharge. More than aesthetics, it is about creating a single, purposefully functional, student-centered hub.

The proposal is currently in the approval process. This page will be updated as the project develops.
