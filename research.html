Linguistics · Translation Studies

Bilingual Evaluation of Tone and Emotional Nuance in Korean–English Translation: Human vs. AI

Twenty Korean–English bilinguals evaluated human and ChatGPT-4o translations of a Korean folktale, rating tone, emotional nuance, and cultural meaning. Participants prioritized emotional authenticity over literal accuracy — and notably, only 60% could identify which translation was AI-generated. The study finds that translation quality depends not on whether a human or machine produced it, but on how each handles specific linguistic and cultural challenges, illuminating the sophisticated metalinguistic awareness bilingual readers bring to evaluation.

Frequency Accelerates Semantic Change: Evidence from 500 Years of Korean

The conventional "law of conformity" holds that frequent words are semantically stable. This study shows that Korean breaks that rule. Using diachronic word embeddings trained on historical corpora spanning the 15th to 20th centuries, the research finds a robust positive correlation between frequency and semantic shift in Korean — high-frequency words change more, not less. The reversal is linked to frequency-driven processes unique to Korean, including grammaticalization, semantic bleaching, and domain shift, suggesting that the frequency–change relationship varies with language typology rather than holding as a universal law.

Ecology · Environmental Science

Characteristics and Species Diversity of S. angulatus Colonies in the Imjin River Basin of the Western Civilian Control Line

Bur cucumber (Sicyos angulatus) is an invasive plant species disrupting ecosystems across South Korea by inhibiting native plant growth and destroying riparian vegetation. This field study analyzed the impact of S. angulatus on species diversity in the Imjin River basin of the Western Civilian Control Line — one of the most ecologically unique landscapes in the world. The study found the species concentrated along riverbanks and forest edges, where damp, sunlit conditions create ideal habitat, and was nearly absent in agricultural flatlands.
